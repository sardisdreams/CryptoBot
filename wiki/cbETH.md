# Coinbase Wrapped Staked Ether (cbETH)

## What it is
cbETH represents ETH staked through Coinbase. It accumulates Ethereum staking rewards (~3-4% APY) and can be traded or used in DeFi. Price trades at a slight premium to ETH (the "staking ratio") that grows over time as rewards accrue.

## Links
- Website: https://www.coinbase.com/cbeth
- Basescan: https://basescan.org/token/0x2Ae3F1Ec7F1F5012CFEab0185bfc7aa3cf0DEc22

## Fundamentals
- Always worth slightly more than ETH due to accumulated staking yield
- Conversion ratio increases over time (~3-4% APY compounding)
- Coinbase charges a ~25% fee on staking rewards (cost embedded in slower ratio growth)
- Competes with Lido's stETH and Rocket Pool's rETH
- cbETH can be unstaked via Coinbase with a standard Ethereum withdrawal delay (~3-5 days)

## Price history
- Tracks ETH price + staking premium
- Ratio started at 1.0 when launched (Aug 2022), now ~1.07+ and growing
- Premium can compress during market stress (liquidity premium disappears)

## Key catalysts to watch
- Ethereum staking rate changes (more stakers = lower yield = slightly less premium)
- Coinbase regulatory events
- ETH price movements (cbETH follows ETH very closely)
- DeFi integrations adding cbETH as collateral

## Trading notes
- Best used as a long-term ETH position that earns yield passively
- Less suitable for short-term trades (premium can be illiquid)
- Spread vs ETH widens in volatile markets — check ratio before trading
- Useful to hold instead of WETH when expecting ETH to hold value long-term

## Risk factors
- Counterparty risk: Coinbase controls the staking
- Smart contract risk (cbETH contract)
- Liquidity risk: less liquid than WETH on Base
- Slashing risk (Ethereum validator penalties — unlikely but possible)
